How to reverse-engineer watch face packets using Android HCI Snoop Log
  1. Enable Bluetooth HCI Snoop: On your Android phone, go to Settings > System > Developer Options. Enable "Enable Bluetooth HCI snoop log" (captures all BLE packets to a file).
  2. Perform Watch Face Sync: Open the official GOBOULT Fit app, select any watch face, and click sync. Let it complete.
  3. Export the Log: Disable snoop logging. Generate a bug report on your device or locate the log file (usually named `btsnoop_hci.log`) in your internal storage or under `/data/misc/bluetooth/logs/`.
  4. Analyze with Wireshark: Transfer the log to your laptop and open it in Wireshark. Filter for `btatt`.
  5. Extract Service and Chars: Look for the handle corresponding to the firmware transfer. You will see writes of 256-byte blocks to characteristic `0xFEE5`, command confirmations on `0xFEE3`, and initial headers written to `0xFEE2`. This will confirm if your firmware requires customized packet headers.
